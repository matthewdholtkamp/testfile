Neuroinflammation / Microglial Activation

Neuroinflammation / Microglial Activation driver hypothesis

Microglial NLRP3 inflammasome activation promotes pro-inflammatory cytokine release. Aquaporin-4 (AQP-4) genetic knock-out exacerbates post-traumatic brain injury (TBI) glymphatic dysfunction, resulting in the accumulation of tau protein, S100β, glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP), and neuron-specific enolase (NSE), which promotes neurofibrillary pathology, neuroinflammation, and neurodegeneration. GAS6 in 3D-sEVs drives M1 to M2 microglial polarization.
